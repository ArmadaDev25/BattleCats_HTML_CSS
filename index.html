Battle Cats

Mikeshake

Mikeshake is a whirlwind of fur and fury, a warrior kitten feared not for his size, but for his signature move—the Shake of Doom. Clad in a cape made from a torn sock and eyes that gleam with untamed mischief, Mikeshake doesn’t just fight—he shakes. With a pounce and a purr that masks impending chaos, he grabs hold of his enemies and thrashes them side to side in a blur of claws, fluff, and indignation. Goblins, ogres, even seasoned knights have found themselves flung like rag dolls, dazed and disheveled, wondering how such a cute creature could cause such catastrophic shaking. Legends say if you hear a furious jingling bell and feel the ground tremble slightly... Mikeshake is coming.

Bonkin' Billy

Bonkin’ Billy is no ordinary kitten—he’s a pint-sized powerhouse of pure bonk energy. With his scrappy fur, a dented thimble for a helmet, and an ironclad stare that could melt the bravest knight’s resolve, Billy charges into battle on stubby paws, wielding his legendary Bonk Bat: a tiny club carved from ancient driftwood and justice. He doesn't meow—he declares. With a single bound and a whirling swing, he bonks enemies square on the noggin, leaving them woozy, wondering what just happened, and maybe a little impressed. Tales of his heroic bonkings echo across kingdoms, where even dragons whisper his name with caution: “That’s Bonkin’ Billy... hide your helmets.”

Wild Willy

Don’t let the name fool you—Wild Willy isn’t just wild by name, he’s wild by nature. A blur of claws, grit, and chaotic energy, this scrappy warrior kitten hails from the untamed alleyways of Clawford, where he learned to fight before he could meow. With a torn bandana around his neck and battle scars etched into his fur, Wild Willy darts through the battlefield like a streak of living lightning. He fights dirty, fast, and fearlessly, using unpredictable moves like the Whisker Whip and the Tailspin Takedown to leave foes reeling. No plan survives contact with Wild Willy—not even his own. He’s a one-kitten storm, and when the dust settles, only the boldest dare stand where Wild Willy has passed.
